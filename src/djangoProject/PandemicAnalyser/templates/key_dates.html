Key Dates during Covid-19 Pandemic

This page shows the sentiment at key dates and compares with the average sentiment of the week, month and year the key date occurred in.
The information is shown as a barchart. The polarity for the key date is shown as a green bar, the week is shown in yellow, the month is shown in purple and the year is shown in red.

The key dates were from The Guardian Timeline of Covid-19 Pandemic and CNN Pandemic Fast Facts


7 April 2020

Prime Minister Boris Johns is moved to intensive care after testing positive for Covid-19 11 days prior.

2 August 2020

The rate of new infections being detected globally has plateaued at more than 200,000 cases a day. Spain, Germany, Italy and the UK are detecting fewer than 50 positive cases per 100,000 people, their lowest rates since the beginning of the outbreak.

22 September 2020

New Covid restrictions are announced in the UK and around Europe - Boris Johnson to set out limited nationwide coronavirus restrictions on Tuesday, covid cases in the UK are 6400 that day. Covid cases in Europe increase quickly after the drop in the summer months.

{#21-27 september 2020#}

2 October 2020

US President Donald Trump tests positive for Coronavirus. The night of second of October he tweeted: “Tonight, @FLOTUS and I tested positive for Covid-19. We will begin our quarantine and recovery process immediately.”. By this time, the total deaths in the US is 209,000.

{#28 September - 4 October 2020#}

9 November 2020

A second wave of cases is occurring in Europe as more countries return to lockdown and restrictions. England has returned to a stringent lockdown. France has closed bars and restaurants and requires citizens to seek permission to leave their homes. In Spain, bars and restaurants remain open until midnight. Daily US cases exceed 130,000 a day, and 50 million worldwide infections are reported.

{#9-15 November 2020#}

23 November 2020

AstraZeneca and Oxford University report their vaccine candidate has at least 61% efficacy and up to 90%. Pfizer and Moderna announced over the previous fortnight that their formulations halted symptoms in up to 95% of trial participants.

23 August 2021

The FDA grants full approval to the Pfizer/BioNTech Covid-19 vaccine for people age 16 and older, making it the first coronavirus vaccine approved by the FDA.

19 November 2021

The FDA authorizes boosters of the Pfizer/BioNTech and Moderna Covid-19 vaccines for all adults. The same day, the CDC also endorses boosters for all adults.

The FDA grants full approval to Moderna’s Covid-19 vaccine for those ages 18 and older. This is the second coronavirus vaccine given full approval by the FDA.
