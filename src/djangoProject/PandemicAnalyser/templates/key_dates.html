Key Dates during Covid-19 Pandemic

This page shows the sentiment at key dates and compares with the average sentiment during the month

Key dates in 2020:

March 23, 2020: The United Kingdom announces a nationwide lockdown
April 2nd - Global cases of COVID-19 exceed one million
April 10th - COVID-19 deaths pass 100,000
April 15 - Cases pass 2 million
May 1st - US Food and Drug Administration issued emergency approval for the hepatitis and Ebola treatment, Remdesivir, to be used as COVID-19 treatment.
15 May - Death toll passes 300,000
28 June - global cases passed 10 million
16 November - Moderna announced its Phase 3 trial data showed the vaccine to be more than 94% effective
2 December - UK became the first country to approve the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine

2021:

15 January - the world passed 2 million COVID-19 deaths
30 Jan - marked a year since the WHO declared the pandemic a public health emergency of international concern
30 March - WHO released findings of a report into origins of the virus that finds it “highly likely” it was transmitted from a bat to humans
19 April - marked the end of a week with 5.2 million cases reported, the highest rate since the pandemic started.
21 April - India recorded 315, 735 new cases on 21 April, the world’s highest recorded single-day total in any country.
11 May - A new coronavirus strain first detected - the Delta variant first detected in India - was declared a "variant of concern"
May 19 - The United States lifts mask mandates for fully vaccinated individuals
7 July - confirmed COVID-19 deaths around the world pass the 4 million milestone.
23 August - the US Food and Drug Administration approve the first COVID-19 vaccinations for people under 16 years of age
September 22 - global death toll from COVID-19 reaches 4.7 million, according to Johns Hopkins University. October 21 - India passed the milestone of administering 1 billion COVID-19 vaccinations
November 1st - The global death toll from the pandemic surpassed 5 million lives

2022:

January 30th - 2 year anniversary since the WHO declared the pandemic a public health emergency of international concern
